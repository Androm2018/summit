About this hill

Ben Nevis is the highest mountain in the British Isles at 1,345 metres. Located near Fort William in the Lochaber area, it attracts around 125,000 ascents every year. The summit plateau — about 100 acres — contains the ruins of an observatory staffed from 1883 to 1904. The most popular route is the Mountain Track ascending from Glen Nevis.
